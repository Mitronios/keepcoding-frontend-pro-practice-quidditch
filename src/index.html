Quidditch Champions XVIII

About the games

The Quidditch World Cup has been held every four years since 1473. The competition is held between Quidditch teams representing countries around the world.

The number of participating countries varies from tournament to tournament. Any country can enter a team within twelve months of the previous final.

Classification

QUALIFICATION Sixteen groups are formed into which the teams are divided. Each team plays against all other teams in its group for a period of two years. During the group stage, there is a limit of four hours of play for each player to prevent exhaustion.

Because the game ends after four hours of play and the Snitch is not caught, the result is decided by goals. A win earns two points. In addition to these two points, a win by 150 points earns five points, at 100 points an extra three points, and at 50 points an extra point.

If two teams are tied on points, they are separated by whichever team catches the Snitch most often or most quickly during their matches. The sixteen teams that have finished at the top of the sixteen groups qualify for the World Cup.
